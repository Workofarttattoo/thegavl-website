Intelligence From Open Sources

OSINT Investigation module systematically gathers, analyzes, and connects evidence from publicly available sources: social media, business databases, regulatory filings, news archives, property records, and web resources. Build stronger cases by uncovering evidence opposing counsel may have missed.

Digital Footprint Analysis
Analyze social media profiles, digital history, and online presence. Identify contradictions between testimony and public behavior.
Business Intelligence Gathering
Access SEC filings, corporate records, and business databases. Understand financial capacity and settlement authority.
Network Analysis
Map relationships between parties, witnesses, and organizations. Identify undisclosed conflicts of interest and connections.
Timeline Reconstruction
Build detailed timelines using news reports, social media posts, and public records. Verify or contradict party claims.
Geolocation Intelligence
Use location data from public sources and geotags. Verify alibis or establish proximity to relevant locations and events.
Pattern Recognition
Identify patterns in public behavior, financial transactions, and communications. Uncover schemes and cover-up attempts.

How It Works

OSINT Investigation applies systematic intelligence gathering:

  1. Identifies relevant publicly available data sources for your case
  2. Systematically gathers information from multiple sources
  3. Cross-references and verifies information for reliability
  4. Analyzes patterns, connections, and contradictions
  5. Generates actionable intelligence for case strategy

Use Cases

Witness Impeachment
Find public statements, social media posts, or published records that contradict witness testimony or credibility.
Evidence Discovery
Identify documents, photos, and statements accessible publicly that should be discovered or obtained through discovery.
Party Profile Development
Build comprehensive profiles of opposing parties using public information. Understand motivations and likely strategies.
Financial Analysis
Assess opponent's financial situation using SEC filings, property records, and business databases for settlement positioning.
Expert Witness Vetting
Research expert credentials, published work, and prior testimony. Identify experts with conflicts or consistency issues.
Regulatory Compliance Review
Analyze public regulatory records and filings. Identify violations, sanctions, or prior similar conduct.
